## Mini-Drama Format (30-90 seconds)

### Structure
- **HOOK** (0-3s): Jarring visual or line that stops the scroll. Must work silent.
- **SETUP** (3-15s): Establish situation and characters. Max 2 characters introduced.
- **ESCALATION** (15-45s): Conflict builds with 2-3 beats. Each beat raises stakes.
- **PUNCHLINE** (45-70s): Subverted expectation. The twist must be visual, not just verbal.
- **TAG** (optional, 70-90s): Final reaction or callback. Often a single reaction shot.

### Scene Count
3-8 scenes. Fewer is better. Each scene change is a hard cut.

### Character Rules
- Max 2-3 characters (AI generation struggles with more)
- Each character needs a detailed visual description for consistency
- At least one character should be non-human or absurd (higher virality)
- Voice styles must be distinct between characters

### Dialogue Rules
- Max 8 words per line
- Subtext > exposition (show don't tell)
- At least one highly quotable line (the one people screenshot)
- Silence/reactions are valid "dialogue" — often funnier than words

### Audio
- Underscore music that builds tension through escalation
- SFX on punchline for emphasis (record scratch, boom, vine boom)
- Music sting on tag

### Generation Requirements
- character_consistency: true (always for mini-drama)
- lip_sync_needed: true (if any character has dialogue)
- Preferred video model: Kling or Veo3 (best character motion)
- Preferred image model: Flux Kontext (character reference support)
